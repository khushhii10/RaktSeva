How to Donate Blood

Follow these simple steps to donate blood and save lives.

  1. Find a blood donation center near you.
  2. Register at the donation center and fill out a health screening form.
  3. Undergo a mini-physical exam (blood pressure, temperature, hemoglobin check).
  4. Donate the blood! The process takes about 10-15 minutes.
  5. Rest and hydrate after your donation.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Must be between 18 and 65 years of age.
  • Weigh at least 50 kg (110 lbs).
  • Free of any infections or medical conditions that can be transmitted through blood.
  • In good health and feeling well on the day of donation.
  • Wait for at least 56 days between whole blood donations.

Blood Donation Myths & Facts

Myth: Donating blood is painful.

Fact: The donation process is relatively painless and only takes a few minutes.

Myth: I can’t donate blood if I have a medical condition.

Fact: Most conditions won’t disqualify you. Speak with a doctor or blood donation staff for more details.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • How often can I donate blood? You can donate whole blood every 56 days.
  • Is it safe to donate blood? Yes, blood donation is safe, and you are closely monitored throughout the process.
  • What happens to the donated blood? It is tested, processed, and used for patients in need.
